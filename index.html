About me

In my research, I focus on developing and applying advanced mathematical and statistical modeling techniques to address challenges in understanding and managing complex systems. Through a series of case studies in epidemiology, biostatistics, and environmental management, I investigate the importance of uncertainty quantification and model selection in providing reliable insights for decision-making. My research explores various modeling approaches, such as stochastic differential equations for disease spread, Bayesian semiparametric methods for diagnostic test evaluation, optimal experimental design for biological systems, and the value of information concept for water quality monitoring. By considering the specific demands and constraints of each problem, I aim to demonstrate the value of tailoring modeling strategies to the unique characteristics of the systems under study. Ultimately, my goal is to contribute to the advancement of complex systems analysis by providing a framework for case study-driven model development and uncertainty quantification that can be applied across different domains. Through my work, I hope to highlight the importance of effective collaboration between modelers, subject matter experts, and decision-makers in addressing real-world challenges.
